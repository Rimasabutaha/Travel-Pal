Safari.
Riyadh Desert

A safari in the Riyadh desert is a mesmerizing journey into the heart of the Arabian Peninsula, where every dune conceals a tale, and every sunset paints the sands in a new palette of colors. You'll set out on an adventure led by seasoned desert guides, tracing the footsteps of ancient caravans. Riding on the back of a camel or in a 4x4 vehicle, you'll explore hidden oases, encounter desert wildlife, and immerse yourself in the age-old traditions of Arabian hospitality.

Snow Sledding.
Jabal Al-Lawz

Snow sledding on Jabal Al-Lawz is an exhilarating adventure in Saudi Arabia. Located in the Asir region, this unique mountain offers an unexpected treat during the winter months when it's covered in snow. Whether you're a seasoned snow sports enthusiast or just looking for some frosty fun, sliding down these slopes is a thrilling experience against the backdrop of Saudi Arabia's desert climate.

Camping.
The Edge of the World

Camping on the Edge of the World, located just outside Riyadh, is a surreal experience that transports you to a realm of unmatched natural beauty and solitude. Perched on the precipice of a massive escarpment, this breathtaking campsite offers unobstructed views of a seemingly endless desert landscape Camping here is an opportunity to reconnect with nature and savor the magic of the Saudi Arabian desert in all its majestic glory.

Exploring.
Farasan Island

Touring the Farasan Islands in the Red Sea is a journey into an unspoiled natural paradise. Located off the southwestern coast of Saudi Arabia, these islands offer vibrant coral reefs, tranquil beaches, and rich cultural heritage. Keep an eye out for resident dolphins in the surrounding waters, adding an element of enchantment to your island adventure. It's an irresistible escape, where nature and history converge for a breathtaking experience.

Hiking.
Wadi Al-Disah

Hiking in Wadi Disah that's found in Tabuk is a journey into the heart of nature's grandeur. This valley, is a hidden gem renowned for its dramatic landscapes and pristine surroundings. As you step into this remote wilderness, you'll find yourself enveloped by soaring cliffs, intricate rock formations, and a tranquil stream that meanders through the canyon. The hike is an enchanting blend of adventure and serenity.

Culture.
Gingerbread Village

Exploring the culture of the Gingerbread Village in Rijal Almaa is a captivating journey through the history and traditions of Saudi Arabia's Asir region. The village's unique gingerbread-like architecture, intricate woodwork, and cobblestone streets provide a living museum of local heritage. While wandering through this village, you'll experience warm hospitality, witness age-old customs, and immerse yourself in Asir's enduring traditions.
